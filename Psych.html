Psychological Disorders throughout History

In Greece, Hippocrates believed mental illnesses to be the results of natural, not supernatural, causes. Galen believed mental disorders to stem from both physical and psychological causes. In China, Chung Ching studied organ pathologies and stress-based psychology. Ching believed that erratic behaviors were the result of mental disorders.

The Middle Ages

In the Middle Ages, abnormal behaviors were thought to result from demonic possessions. This common belief caused humanity's progress on understanding psychology to backtrack dramatically.
